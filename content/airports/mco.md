# MCO Airport Guide

> Fast, no-fluff reference for clearing Orlando's two terminals with minimal walking and waiting.

## Quick Facts
- Terminals **A**, **B**, and **C** — C opened 2022 as a separate facility linked via Terminal Link
- Largest carriers include **Southwest, Delta, Spirit, Frontier, and American**; **JetBlue** uses Terminal C only
- TSA PreCheck and CLEAR lanes at all terminals
- No universal airside link between A/B and C — connections may require re-clearing security
- **Brightline** intercity rail serves the station adjacent to Terminal C
- **MCT (A ↔ B):** landside walk + re-security — budget **90 min** minimum on separate tickets.
- **Wi‑Fi:** Free **MCO WiFi** — browser login; sessions expire.
- **Power:** Outlets near Gate 100 (A) and central B gates; charge before peak banks.
- **Water:** Refill fountains post-security in both terminals — bring an empty bottle.

## Security & Screening Tips
- Use the airport app to view live wait times before leaving the car.
- PreCheck and CLEAR are fastest at Terminal A; Terminal B lines move slower.
- Avoid 7-10am and 4-7pm windows; lines rarely exceed 25 minutes outside those blocks.
- Families and large groups should use the dedicated family lane at Terminal A.

## Best Airport Tricks & Hacks
- Park in Terminal A garage and walk the connector if your flight is in B—often faster than the shuttle.
- Grab food landside at **Shake Shack (Terminal C Palm Court)** or **Qdoba (B)** before security; airside choices are weak.
- Download the MCO app and enable push alerts for gate changes and security times.
- CLEAR members can skip the PreCheck line entirely at both terminals during peaks.
- If connecting from an airside arrival, exit security, walk the connector, and re-clear rather than waiting for slow shuttles.
- Rental car return is fast; follow signs to the new consolidated facility instead of older lots.
- Early morning flights (before 7am) often have empty security—arrive 60 minutes early.

## Terminals & Navigation
- Terminal A handles most low-cost carriers including Spirit; Terminal B serves Delta, United, and American; **Terminal C** serves JetBlue and other carriers.
- The A-to-B walkway takes 12-15 minutes; moving walkways help but are often crowded.
- All gates are airside; once you clear security you cannot switch terminals without exiting.
- Common mistake: parking in the wrong garage—double-check your airline before choosing A or B.

## Lounges, Food & Amenities
- The Club at MCO in Terminal B is the only decent lounge; avoid if traveling basic economy.
- Best quick bites are landside: **Shake Shack (Terminal C)** and **Qdoba (B)**.
- Quietest seating is near Gate 100 in Terminal A after 8pm.
- No standout premium lounges for most travelers; plan to eat before security.

## Ground Transport & Parking
- Economy parking is cheapest but requires a shuttle; garage parking is worth the extra $8-10 for speed.
- Uber/Lyft pickup is on Level 2 of both terminals—fastest exit during non-peak hours.
- Avoid the old cell phone lot; use the new one near the rental car center for shorter waits.
- **Brightline** intercity rail serves MCO at the train station adjacent to Terminal C (Terminal Link from A/B).
- Lynx buses 11 and 51 also serve the airport for local public transport.
