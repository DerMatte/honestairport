# SFO Airport Guide

> SFO rewards travelers who know the AirTrain shortcuts, BART link, and United-heavy gate patterns.

## Quick Facts
- Four terminals (**1**, **2**, **3**, **International**) connected post-security and by frequent AirTrain; gate areas are lettered **A–G** (e.g. United uses **Terminal 3** E/F gates; International Terminal has **A** and **G** gates).
- **MCT (same ticket, practical floor):**
  - **Adjacent terminals** (T2↔T3 or T3↔International): **60 min** floor — walk is **8–10 min** post-security.
  - **Cross-terminal** (T1↔International): **75–90 min** including possible re-security.
  - **International arrival → domestic:** full re-screen — **2 h** minimum.
- United operates the majority of flights from **Terminal 3** (E/F gates) and the International Terminal **G** gates.
- BART station sits directly beneath the International Terminal for seamless regional access.
- TSA PreCheck lanes exist in every terminal; CLEAR is also widespread.
- International arrivals require full re-screening for most domestic connections.
- Free Wi-Fi and plentiful power outlets throughout all terminals.
- **Water:** Refill stations post-security in Terminal 2 and International Terminal — bring an empty bottle.

## Security & Screening Tips
- PreCheck and CLEAR cut wait times to under 10 minutes at all terminals.
- Avoid main checkpoint rushes 6-9 AM and 4-7 PM; use the less crowded International Terminal G-side PreCheck when possible.
- Families and oversized bags slow International Terminal G-side lines most; arrive earlier if traveling with either.
- No need to remove shoes or laptops in PreCheck lanes at SFO.

## Best Airport Tricks & Hacks
- Walk from International Terminal **G** gates to **A** gates (or Terminal 3 to International Terminal) instead of riding AirTrain when your connection is under 25 minutes.
- Download the MySFO app and enable gate alerts; United changes gates more than any other carrier here.
- Grab food in Terminal 2 before security if your flight departs from Terminal 3 or the International Terminal; options are better and less crowded.
- Use the BART station for any Bay Area destination; it beats rideshares on price and traffic.
- Park in the domestic garage closest to your terminal rather than the international garage for quick exits.
- Check the AirTrain status board before boarding; it occasionally skips stops during maintenance.
- International Terminal has the quietest seating areas post-security after 10 PM when most long-haul flights have departed.

## Terminals & Navigation
- All terminals connect post-security except for some international arrivals that force a security re-clear.
- AirTrain runs every 4 minutes; use it for Terminal 1 hops or rental-car loops only.
- Walking time between International Terminal G and A gates is 8-10 minutes; Terminal 3 to International Terminal is 12-15 minutes.
- Common mistake: riding AirTrain when adjacent terminals are faster on foot.

## Lounges, Food & Amenities
- United Club in International Terminal **G** gates offers solid food and showers; expect crowds during bank departures.
- Terminal 2 pre-security has the best casual dining: The Plant Cafe and Napa Farms Market.
- Quiet seating clusters exist near Gate G3 (International Terminal) and in the International Terminal upper level after 9 PM.
- Avoid the food court in Terminal 1 after 8 PM; most outlets close early.

## Ground Transport & Parking
- BART is fastest and cheapest to downtown or East Bay; trains run every 10-20 minutes.
- Rideshares pick up on the departures level; allow 15 extra minutes during rush hours.
- Short-term parking in the closest domestic garage costs $3 for the first 30 minutes, then $6 per hour.
- Cell phone lot is free and well-marked; use it instead of circling.
