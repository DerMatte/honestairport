# JED Airport Guide

> Practical, no-fluff guide to moving through Jeddah’s main airport with minimal stress and lost time.

## Quick Facts
- Three passenger terminals (T1 Saudia, T2 LCCs, T3 international) plus separate Hajj terminal.
- Saudia is the dominant carrier; expect tight connections on its metal.
- Security is strict on liquids and electronics; prepare for manual bag searches.
- Free Wi-Fi exists but is unreliable in T2 and T3 after 10 pm.
- Currency exchange and SIM kiosks are only landside; handle both before security.
- Taxi and ride-hail pickup is well organized but surges during late-night arrivals.

## Security & Screening Tips
- Remove all electronics and liquids; agents frequently open every bag.
- Women traveling alone or in groups should expect additional questioning at immigration.
- Peak congestion hits 04:00–07:00 and 20:00–23:00; avoid these windows if possible.
- No TSA PreCheck equivalent; arrive with printed boarding pass to speed manual checks.

## Best Airport Tricks & Hacks
- Download the GACA and Saudia apps before arrival; both push gate changes faster than screens.
- If connecting on Saudia, stay airside and use the internal shuttle between T1 piers—saves 15 minutes versus exiting.
- Buy a STC or Mobily SIM landside immediately after immigration; data is cheap and more reliable than Wi-Fi.
- For long layovers, the T1 mosque on the upper level is the quietest spot in the airport.
- Carry small SAR notes for porters and restroom attendants; cards are rarely accepted.
- When departing on a late Saudia flight, check T1 departures level for the 24-hour Al Baik branch before security.
- Avoid the official taxi desk at arrivals if you have a local SIM—Careem and Uber are 30-40% cheaper.

## Terminals & Navigation
- T1 is modern and compact; walking time between farthest gates is under 12 minutes.
- T2 and T3 require a free shuttle that runs every 15 minutes; factor in 20 minutes minimum.
- Common mistake: assuming all Saudia flights use the same pier—double-check the gate on the app.

## Lounges, Food & Amenities
- Saudia Al Fursan Lounge in T1 is the only worthwhile paid option; food is average but seating is reliable.
- Al Baik landside in T1 and T2 beats every airside restaurant for speed and price.
- Quietest seating is the upper-level prayer area in T1 or the far end of T3 near gate 3-15.

## Ground Transport & Parking
- Careem and Uber pickup is clearly signed on arrivals level; expect 15-25 SAR to central Jeddah.
- Official taxis add a fixed 50 SAR surcharge; use only if ride-hail surge is extreme.
- Short-term parking is expensive; long-term lots are cheaper but require a shuttle.
