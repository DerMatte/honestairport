# JFK Airport Guide

> The most important things to know before you fly through New York’s busiest airport.

## Quick Facts
- **IATA**: JFK
- **Location**: Queens, New York City
- **Terminals**: **1** (many international carriers), **4** (Delta + international), **5** (JetBlue + partners), **7** (British Airways, Alaska, others — phasing out as **Terminal 6** opens), **8** (American + oneworld). **Terminal 6** is opening in phases from **spring 2026** on the former T6/T7 site; verify your terminal on every booking — assignments shift during the transition.
- **Critical rule:** JFK terminals are **not** connected airside. Any inter-terminal transfer means **exit security → AirTrain → re-clear TSA** at the next terminal.
- **MCT (same ticket, practical floor):**
  - Same terminal: **60 min** minimum
  - Different terminal, domestic–domestic: **90 min** floor, **2 h** safer in peaks
  - International arrival → domestic connection: **2.5–3 h** (CBP + possible bags + AirTrain + TSA)
  - Separate tickets: **4 h+** minimum

## Security & Screening Tips
- JFK has multiple security checkpoints per terminal. Check your terminal and the airline app for the shortest lines.
- PreCheck and CLEAR are available in most active terminals and usually worth it for frequent flyers.
- 3-1-1 liquids rule is strictly enforced. Use clear quart-sized bags.
- **Peak pain:** Early morning (6–9 a.m.) and late afternoon/evening (4–8 p.m.) — add **20–30 min** buffer for TSA when changing terminals.
- **International arrivals:** Everyone clears **CBP** on first entry to the U.S. Connecting passengers with through-checked bags still re-clear TSA at the departure terminal unless on a specific airline-arranged exception — don't assume otherwise.

## Best Airport Tricks & Hacks
- **Terminal 4 Delta Sky Club**: Often less crowded than others; good for layovers — but useless if you're connecting out of T5/T8 without time to leave and re-enter security elsewhere.
- **AirTrain + Subway hack**: For Manhattan, sometimes faster/cheaper than taxi during rush hour (but watch luggage and stairs).
- **Charging + seating**: Terminal 5 has good power outlets near gates 20–30 and quieter seating areas upstairs.
- **Food tip — Shake Shack**: Only in **T4** (Gates **B23** and **B37**) and **T8** (Gate **12**), both **airside**. You cannot reach them from other terminals without exiting and re-clearing security — order via app at peak meal times when you're already in the right terminal.
- **Terminal 6 transition (2026):** Airlines are moving from T7 to T6 in phases — double-check terminal on your boarding pass the week of travel; pickup zones and check-in counters change during migration.

## Terminals & Navigation
- **No airside shortcuts:** This is the single biggest JFK gotcha. Even T5↔T7 look close on a map — you still ride the **AirTrain** and pass through **TSA again**.
- **AirTrain:** Free between all terminals, runs **24/7** every **5–10 min**; adjacent terminals ~**5–8 min** ride, T1↔T8 ~**20–22 min** end-to-end with station walks.
- **Walking between terminals:** Not possible landside or airside — AirTrain only.
- **Recommended connection buffers (experienced-traveler floor, not airline marketing):**
  - Same terminal: **60 min**
  - Different terminal, one ticket, no immigration: **90 min** floor, **2 h** in peaks
  - International → domestic: **2.5–3 h**
  - Separate tickets: **4 h+**
- **Common mistakes:** Booking a **60 min** connection T4→T5; assuming you can "walk" between terminals; ignoring the T6/T7 migration in 2026.

## Lounges, Food & Amenities
- **Delta (T4):** Sky Club — largest lounge footprint; good for T4 departures only.
- **JetBlue (T5):** Rooftop terrace and multiple dining options; best when you're actually departing T5.
- **American / oneworld (T8):** Admirals Club and partner lounges; Shake Shack near Gate 12 airside.
- **Terminal 1:** Many Star Alliance and international carriers — lounge access varies by airline; long concourse walks add time.
- **Paid / independent:** Some terminals have contract lounges — day-pass rules change; verify before travel.
- **Power:** Charge right after security — gate competition during delays is fierce.
- **Wi‑Fi:** Free JFK Wi‑Fi; registration may be required.

## Ground Transport & Parking
- **AirTrain** connects all terminals to **Jamaica** (LIRR + E/J/Z subway) and **Howard Beach** (A train). Inter-terminal rides are **free**; exiting at Jamaica or Howard Beach costs a fare (verify current rate at [jfkairport.com](https://www.jfkairport.com/)).
- **Taxi:** Flat fare to Manhattan from official stands — confirm zone and surcharges.
- **Uber/Lyft:** Designated pickup areas — surge pricing common during peaks.
- **Lefferts Boulevard lot:** On-airport pickup/drop-off alternative to curbside congestion (via AirTrain).
- **Parking:** Short-stay is expensive — pre-book or use AirTrain + transit for multi-day trips.
