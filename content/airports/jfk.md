# JFK Airport Guide

> New York’s main long-haul gateway — terminal sprawl and zero airside shortcuts mean your connection plan lives or dies on immigration, AirTrain, and TSA queues.

## Quick Facts
- **Terminals (May 2026):** **1** (many international carriers), **4** (Delta + large international hub), **5** (JetBlue), **7** (being phased out as **Terminal 6** opens), **8** (American / oneworld hub). **Terminal 6** — new **1.2M sq ft** facility on the north side — began opening **first gates in spring 2026**; **Terminal 7** operates until demolition for T6 phase 2 ([Port Authority T6 project](https://www.portauthoritybuilds.com/redevelopment/us/en/jfk/planned-projects/terminal-6.html)). Airline assignments are in flux — verify terminal on your ticket before every trip.
- **MCT (same ticket, practical floor):**
  - **Same terminal:** **60–90 min** minimum domestic; **90 min–2 h** international-international in one building.
  - **Terminal change on one ticket:** **2 h** floor, **2.5–3 h** safer — AirTrain + **new TSA** at departure terminal.
  - **International arrival → domestic:** CBP (immigration, baggage, customs) then terminal transfer — **3 h** minimum; **3.5 h+** in peak.
- **Separate tickets:** collect bags, clear customs if arriving internationally, change terminals, check in again. **3–4 h** minimum; no airline protection if you misconnect.
- **Wi‑Fi:** Free airport Wi‑Fi available — registration and session limits apply; details on [jfkairport.com](https://www.jfkairport.com/).
- **Liquids (TSA):** **3-1-1** rule strictly enforced — quart bag, **≤3.4 oz (100 ml)** per container.

## Security & Screening Tips
- **No airside terminal hops:** Unlike some hubs, you **cannot** walk between terminals behind security — every terminal change means **AirTrain (landside) + full TSA** at the departure terminal.
- **Multiple checkpoints per terminal:** Large terminals (especially **T4** and **T8**) have several TSA areas — your airline app or terminal screens usually point to the shortest queue.
- **PreCheck / CLEAR:** Available in most terminals; still queue at morning banks (**06:00–09:00**) and evening peaks.
- **T4 volume:** One of the busiest international terminals in the U.S. — add **20–30 min** TSA buffer vs. smaller airports.
- **Global Entry / Mobile Passport:** Speeds **CBP** on international **arrivals** — does not replace TSA for onward domestic connections.

## Best Airport Tricks & Hacks
- **AirTrain is mandatory between terminals:** Free **within the airport** (fare applies only if you exit to Jamaica/Howard Beach). Follow **AirTrain** signs — allow **10–15 min** train time plus walking in each terminal.
- **T5 ↔ future T6:** T6 shares ground transport with **T5** per Port Authority plans — north-side connections may simplify once T6 is fully open; still assume **separate security** per terminal until official airside links exist (none today).
- **Shake Shack (airside):** **Terminal 4** (Gates **B23** and **B37**) and **Terminal 8** (Concourse B near **Gate 12**) — only if you depart from that terminal; mobile order at rush hour.
- **Delta Sky Club (T4):** Large footprint — can be crowded but reliable for long layovers; shower access depends on membership/cabin.
- **Manhattan on a budget:** **AirTrain** to **Jamaica** → **LIRR** to Penn Station is often faster than a cab in midtown rush hour; with heavy bags, consider **LIRR** vs. **A train** stairs at Howard Beach.
- **Avoid:** Booking **60–90 min** connections across terminals on separate tickets; assuming **T4** and **T5** are walkable behind security (they are not).

## Terminals & Navigation
- **Terminal 1:** Wide-body international carriers (assignments shifting as **T6** opens for some Star/Lufthansa Group airlines in 2026).
- **Terminal 4:** **Delta** hub + many international partners — long concourses; **B** and **A** wings add **10–15 min** walks gate-to-gate.
- **Terminal 5:** **JetBlue** flagship — compact vs. T4; T5 refresh adding concessions through **2026**.
- **Terminal 7 / Terminal 6:** **T7** still operating in 2026 while **T6** gates come online; check whether your flight lists **T6** or **T7** on the day of travel.
- **Terminal 8:** **American** and **oneworld** long-haul — strong lounge and **Shake Shack** on Concourse B.
- **Connection routing:** Arrive → follow **Connecting flights** / **AirTrain** → exit to **AirTrain station** (usually after baggage/immigration if international) → ride to departure terminal → check-in if needed → **TSA** → gate.
- **Recommended buffers:**
  - Same terminal: **60–90 min** (domestic), **90 min–2 h** (international)
  - Inter-terminal, one ticket: **2 h** floor, **2.5–3 h** comfortable
  - International → domestic: **3 h** floor, **3.5 h+** peak
  - Separate tickets: **3–4 h** minimum
- **Common mistakes:** Underestimating **AirTrain + TSA**; booking **LGA/EWR** interchange as a “NYC connection” (different airports entirely).

## Lounges, Food & Amenities
- **T4:** Largest lounge selection (Delta, SkyTeam partners, pay lounges) — best for long layovers if you’re already in T4.
- **T8:** **Admirals Club / Flagship** and **Shake Shack** — solid for oneworld connections without leaving the terminal.
- **T5:** JetBlue **Rooftop Terrace** (when open) and improving food court — good value vs. legacy terminals; **Shake Shack** arriving as part of T5 refresh (check if open on your date).
- **T1 / T6:** Lounge maps changing with **T6** rollout — verify access on your airline’s app.
- **Overrated / traps:** Sit-down restaurants at peak without mobile order; leaving T4 for a T5 lounge (impossible airside); buying adapters at premium prices — **Hudson** or **Best Buy** kiosks exist but cost more than city retail.
- **Power:** T4 and T8 have reasonable outlet density near gates **20–30** (T5) and upper concourses — charge after TSA, not at boarding call.

## Ground Transport & Parking
- **AirTrain JFK:** Links all terminals to **Jamaica** (LIRR to Manhattan/Brooklyn) and **Howard Beach** (A train). **Pay fare** when entering/leaving the system (not free to NYC). Allow **45–75 min** to Midtown depending on mode and time of day.
- **Taxi / ride-hail:** Flat-rate zones and app pickups exist — surge pricing at peaks; can beat transit with **3+ passengers** or late night.
- **Car services:** Shared vans and buses to Manhattan — compare with LIRR for solo travelers.
- **Parking:** On-airport garages are costly for multi-day stays — off-airport lots with shuttle are common; check Port Authority rates for your terminal.
