# JFK Airport Guide

> New York's main international gateway — excellent when terminals align, unforgiving when they don't, and actively reshuffling through 2028.

## Quick Facts
- **Current terminals (May 2026):** **1** (legacy international), **4** (Delta + many international carriers), **5** (JetBlue), **7** (multiple Star/oneworld carriers), **8** (American + partners). **Terminals 2 and 3 are demolished**; replacements are under construction.
- **Redevelopment alert:** The **New Terminal One** (south side) begins **phased opening in 2026**; **Terminal 6** (north side, linked to T5) opens **first gates in 2026**, full build by **2028** ([Port Authority JFK rebuild](https://www.portauthoritybuilds.com/redevelopment/us/en/jfk.html)). **Always confirm terminal on your airline app within 24 hours of departure.**
- **MCT (practical floor):**
  - Same terminal, domestic–domestic: **75–90 min**
  - AirTrain terminal change on one ticket: **2 h** floor, **2.5 h** in peaks (includes re-screening)
  - **International arrival → any onward flight:** **2.5–3 h minimum** (immigration, baggage, recheck, security)
  - Separate tickets: **4+ hours** — full arrival procedures each time
- **AirTrain:** **Free** between all passenger terminals; **$8.75** one-way to **Jamaica** (LIRR/subway) or **Howard Beach** (A train) — contactless/OMNY ([AirTrain JFK](https://www.jfkairport.com/transportation/airtrain)).
- **Wi‑Fi:** Free airport Wi‑Fi in terminals — registration via browser portal (network name varies by concession; look for official JFK/PANYNJ signage).
- **Liquids:** US **3-1-1** rule in carry-ons; stricter re-screening on every terminal change.

## Security & Screening Tips
- **Re-screening is mandatory on terminal changes:** Even on one ticket, changing terminals means **new TSA checkpoint** at the departure terminal ([JFK connections guide](https://www.jfkairport.com/flights/connections-guide)).
- **International connection rule (official):** Arriving internationally, you **must collect checked bags** at baggage claim — even if tagged to your final destination — then **recheck** at the baggage recheck facility before your connecting flight.
- **Construction disruption (May 2026+):** AirTrain inner-loop service modifications can increase waits between **T1, T4, T5, T7, T8** — add **15–30 min** buffer during active work ([AirTrain advisory](https://www.jfkairport.com/transportation/airtrain)).
- **PreCheck / CLEAR:** Available in most terminals; lines still build during morning and evening banks.
- **Peak windows:** Weekday mornings (**06:00–09:00**) and evenings (**16:00–20:00**) are consistently slower; summer and holiday peaks add immigration time for international arrivals.

## Best Airport Tricks & Hacks
- **Operating carrier terminal wins:** Codeshare bookings may show one airline but depart from another terminal — check the **operating carrier** on your boarding pass (e.g. Finnair ticket, American flight → **Terminal 8**).
- **AirTrain, not curbs:** For terminal changes, follow signs to **AirTrain** — free, runs every **5–15 min**, under **10 min** between adjacent terminals when service is normal.
- **T4 internal shuttle:** Delta's **free shuttle bus** connects **T4 concourses** (stops near Gates **A9, B23, B54**) — **no re-screening** within T4 only.
- **Shake Shack (T4 airside):** Two locations in **Concourse B** near Gates **B23** and **B37** — reliable fast food if you're already in T4 after security.
- **Manhattan on a budget:** **AirTrain ($8.75) + E/J/Z subway from Jamaica** beats taxi in rush hour; **LIRR from Jamaica** is faster but pricier. Howard Beach **A train** works for downtown/Brooklyn routes.
- **T5 layover:** JetBlue's **T5** has solid food and retail; **TWA Hotel** is connected airside for long layovers (paid).
- **Avoid:** Booking **60–75 min** connections across terminals; assuming bags interline on **separate tickets**; ignoring **2026 terminal moves** to the new Terminal One or Terminal 6.

## Terminals & Navigation
- **Terminal 4:** Largest terminal — **Delta** hub plus dozens of international carriers. Multiple concourses; use the **internal shuttle** if gates are far apart.
- **Terminal 5:** **JetBlue** hub — will eventually connect to new **Terminal 6** for partner airlines (Lufthansa Group, Air Canada, etc. per Port Authority plans).
- **Terminal 8:** **American Airlines** and oneworld partners — opposite side of the airport from much of T4.
- **Terminal 1:** Legacy building; many carriers will migrate to the **New Terminal One** during **2026** phased opening.
- **Terminal 7:** Scheduled for replacement by **Terminal 6**; airlines transitioning through **2026–2028**.
- **AirTrain routing:** All terminals have AirTrain stations; allow **10–20 min** terminal-to-terminal including walk to/from platform.
- **Recommended connection buffers:**
  - Same terminal (e.g. T4→T4): **75–90 min**
  - Cross-terminal domestic on one ticket: **2 h** floor, **2.5 h** comfortable
  - International arrival → domestic/international: **2.5–3 h** floor
  - Separate tickets: **4+ hours**

## Lounges Food & Amenities
- **Terminal 4 lounges:** **Delta Sky Club** (Concourses A & B), **Delta One Lounge**, **Centurion**, **Chase Sapphire**, **Virgin Atlantic Clubhouse**, and others ([JFK T4](https://www.jfkairport.com/explore-jfk/terminals/terminal-4)) — strongest lounge cluster at JFK today.
- **Terminal 5:** JetBlue **Rooftop Terrace** (when open) and partner lounges — good for JetBlue-centric layovers.
- **Terminal 8:** **American Admirals Club** and **Flagship Lounge** for eligible passengers.
- **Food — actually worth it:** **Shake Shack** (T4, B23/B37); **T4 Five Borough Food Hall** for NYC-style variety; **TWA Hotel** dining if you have time for an airside detour.
- **Power:** Gate areas in renovated T4 and T5 generally have outlets — charge right after security, not at boarding.
- **Water:** Limited airside refill compared to European hubs; bring an empty bottle and fill where fountains exist landside.
- **Overrated / traps:** Generic terminal grab-and-go at premium prices when you have lounge access in T4; tight connections that require T4↔T8 cross-field AirTrain rides.

## Ground Transport & Parking
- **Fastest to Manhattan (off-peak):** **LIRR from Jamaica** (~20 min to Penn Station) + AirTrain — check weekend/night schedules.
- **Cheapest to Manhattan:** **AirTrain ($8.75) + subway** from Jamaica (E train) or Howard Beach (A train).
- **Taxi / rideshare:** Flat-rate and metered options from official stands; **surge pricing** common peak hours. Road time **45–75+ min** to Midtown in traffic.
- **Between JFK and Newark/LGA:** Not a quick hop — budget **90+ min** and separate security; consider single-airport itineraries during construction.
- **Parking:** On-airport lots via AirTrain; **Lefferts Blvd** for long-term. Pre-book when possible during peak travel.
- **Construction traffic:** Roadway closures and reroutes are frequent through **2028** — PANYNJ publishes [alerts](https://www.jfkairport.com/) daily.
