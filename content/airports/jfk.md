# JFK Airport Guide

> The most important things to know before you fly through New York’s busiest airport.

## Quick Facts
- **Terminals:** **1** (many international carriers), **4** (Delta hub + partners), **5** (JetBlue hub), **7** (multiple international airlines), **8** (American + partners). Terminals 2 and 3 are demolished. **Terminal 6** is under construction with first gates expected in 2026.
- **Critical:** JFK terminals have **no airside walkways** between buildings. Any terminal change means exit security → AirTrain → **re-clear TSA**.
- **MCT (same ticket):** Same terminal: **60 min** floor. Terminal change: **90 min** minimum; **2 hours** safer for international arrivals with immigration.
- **Separate tickets:** Collect bags, change terminals landside, check in again — budget **3+ hours**.
- **Wi‑Fi:** Free airport Wi‑Fi available in all terminals (registration may be required).
- **Liquids:** Standard TSA **3-1-1** rule — 3.4 oz (100 ml) containers in one quart-sized clear bag.

## Security & Screening Tips
- **Re-screening on connections:** JFK’s official connections guide confirms you **must go through TSA again** when your connecting flight departs from a different terminal.
- **Multiple checkpoints per terminal:** Check your airline app for the shortest line — some terminals have more than one lane.
- **PreCheck and CLEAR:** Available in most terminals; PreCheck is usually the better value for frequent flyers.
- **Peak pain windows:** Weekday mornings (06:00–09:00) and international arrival banks (14:00–18:00) are consistently slow.
- **Construction:** The $19B JFK redevelopment causes frequent roadway and terminal-area disruptions — check alerts before travel.

## Best Airport Tricks & Hacks
- **Confirm operating carrier terminal:** Codeshare tickets often show the marketing airline’s terminal, but you must go to the **operating carrier’s terminal**. Example: a Finnair ticket operated by American Airlines uses **Terminal 8**, not Terminal 1.
- **AirTrain is free between terminals:** Ride the AirTrain loop to your departure terminal, then allow time for TSA. Full loop takes ~15–25 minutes.
- **Manhattan on a budget:** Subway **E** from Jamaica Station (via AirTrain from JFK) or **A** from Howard Beach — combined AirTrain + subway fare is **$11.75** per MTA guidance. Often beats taxi during rush hour; awkward with heavy luggage.
- **LIRR from Jamaica:** Faster to Penn Station or Grand Central than subway — higher fare, check MTA/LIRR schedules.
- **Shake Shack:** Airside in **Terminal 4** (near gates B23 and B37) and **Terminal 8** (near gate 12). Order via app to skip lines.
- **T5 outdoor deck:** JetBlue’s Terminal 5 has a post-security **rooftop deck** — one of the few outdoor airside spaces at JFK; good for long layovers if you are already in T5.
- **Rideshare pickup:** Pickup locations vary with construction — check JFK alerts and your app the day of travel; remote lots and shuttles are used during peak roadwork.

## Terminals & Navigation
- **Layout:** Five active terminals on the outside of a rectangular loop; AirTrain tracks define the loop. Vehicle access and parking are on the inside (landside).
- **AirTrain station levels vary by terminal:**
  - T1: Levels 1 and 4
  - T4: Level 3
  - T5: Levels 1 and 4
  - T7: Levels 1 and 2
  - T8: Levels 1 and 2
- **Walking between terminals:** Possible on sidewalks for adjacent terminals (e.g., T4↔T5) but **not recommended** with luggage — use AirTrain.
- **T5 renovation:** JetBlue is upgrading T5 through 2026 — some areas may be closed; allow extra time.
- **Recommended connection buffers:**
  - Same terminal, domestic: **60 min** minimum
  - Terminal change, domestic: **90 min** minimum
  - International arrival → domestic connection: **2+ hours**
  - Separate tickets: **3+ hours**

## Lounges, Food & Amenities
- **Terminal 4 (best lounge density):**
  - **Delta Sky Club:** Two locations — near Gate A7 (Concourse A) and Gate B31 (Concourse B); ~15 min walk apart.
  - **Delta One Lounge:** Between Concourses A and B — **Delta One ticket or select partner premium cabins only** (not Sky Club membership).
  - **Centurion Lounge, Capital One Lounge, Chase Sapphire Lounge:** Premium credit-card lounges — verify access rules before travel.
- **Terminal 5:** JetBlue **Rooftop and More** (part of JetBlue’s T5 experience) for eligible passengers; limited third-party lounge options.
- **Terminal 8:** American **Admirals Club** and **Flagship Lounge** (Flagship for premium cabin/select status).
- **Terminal 1:** Air France, Lufthansa, and other airline-specific lounges — access depends on carrier and cabin.
- **Food — reliable picks:**
  - **Shake Shack (T4, T8):** Best-known fast option; long lines at peaks without app ordering.
  - **T4:** Widest dining variety — sit-down and grab-and-go across both concourses.
- **Power:** T5 has charging stations throughout; T4 Concourse B gates in the 30s have outlets — grab one early during delays.
- **Water:** Few refill stations compared to newer airports — bring an empty bottle and fill where stations exist, or buy airside.

## Ground Transport & Parking
- **AirTrain JFK:** Connects all terminals to **Jamaica Station** (LIRR + subway E/J/Z) and **Howard Beach** (subway A). **Free** between terminals; **$8.75** when entering/exiting at Jamaica or Howard Beach.
- **Taxi:** Flat fare to Manhattan (check current TLC rate before travel); can exceed **60 min** in rush hour on Van Wyck/Belt Parkway.
- **Uber/Lyft:** Organized but subject to construction-related remote pickup lots — verify location in app on arrival day.
- **Parking:** Terminal garages exist for T1 (Green), T4 (Blue), T5 (Yellow), T8 (Red). T1 and T7 lots may be closed during construction — JFK site lists current availability.
- **Lefferts Blvd lot:** On-airport cell phone / pickup-drop-off lot at the Lefferts Blvd AirTrain station — alternative to curbside congestion.
