# PEK Airport Guide

> This page gives the fastest path through Beijing Capital's three terminals, security bottlenecks, and ground links.

## Quick Facts
- **T2 + West Satellite (former T1):** Check-in and security at **T2**; gates **A01–A16** in the West Satellite reached via airside corridor (opened **18 Jun 2026**) or shuttle.
- **T3:** Largest terminal — most long-haul and SkyTeam; **no airside connection to T2**.
- All passengers face full security screening regardless of connection.
- **MCT (same ticket):** **90 min** T2↔T3 minimum including landside shuttle and re-security; **2 h** safer.
- High-speed Airport Express reaches Dongzhimen in 30 minutes for ¥25.
- No TSA PreCheck equivalent; arrive early for manual checks.
- English signage is adequate but WeChat is required for many services.
- **Wi‑Fi:** Free airport Wi‑Fi — Chinese phone verification often required for full speed.
- **Power:** Charging area behind T3 gate 35; scarce elsewhere — charge landside if you have time.
- **Water:** Refill fountains in T3 post-security; limited in T1/T2.

## Security & Screening Tips
- Expect 60-90 minutes at peak; remove all electronics and liquids.
- Power banks over 100Wh are banned; declare smaller ones.
- Use the T3 security lanes on the far left for slightly shorter queues.
- Avoid the central T3 checkpoint between 7-9 AM.

## Best Airport Tricks & Hacks
- Download the BCIA app and bind your passport before arrival for faster status.
- Landside T3 has superior food; eat before clearing security.
- Free luggage wrapping stations sit near T3 check-in rows 1-10.
- T2↔T3 connections require the **landside shuttle** — there is no airside walkway between T2 and T3.
- Charge devices at the quiet seating area behind T3 gate 35.
- Book Airport Express tickets via WeChat to skip the machine line.
- If delayed, the capsule hotel in T3 basement offers 4-hour blocks for ¥200.

## Terminals & Navigation
- West Satellite (former T1) connects airside to **T2 only** via corridor or shuttle.
- **T3** is **2 km** from T2 — free landside shuttle every **10 min**; no airside link.
- Follow yellow signs for international connections; blue for domestic.
- Common mistake: assuming T2 and T3 are walkable airside — they are not.

## Lounges, Food & Amenities
- Air China Lounge in T3 is the best paid option with showers.
- Landside T3 has solid local food at Taste of Beijing and Din Tai Fung.
- Quietest seating is the mezzanine level above T3 gates 20-30.
- Avoid the crowded food court directly after security in T3.

## Ground Transport & Parking
- Airport Express is fastest and cheapest to central Beijing.
- Taxi queue outside T3 takes 20-40 minutes at peak; use Didi app instead.
- Short-term parking costs ¥10 per hour for first 3 hours.
- Book private transfer 48 hours ahead during major holidays.
