# LAX Airport Guide

> Practical, no-fluff information with an emphasis on actually useful tips and tricks.

## Quick Facts
- **IATA**: LAX
- **Location**: Los Angeles, California
- **Terminals**: 1–8 + Tom Bradley International Terminal (TBIT / Terminal B)
- **Airside connections**: Since 2023, ticketed passengers can walk between **all** terminals post-security without re-clearing TSA — total path is roughly **2 miles** from T1 to T8 ([LAX inter-terminal connections](https://www.flylax.com/inter-terminal-connections)).
- **MCT (same ticket, practical floor):** Same terminal **60 min** minimum; terminal change airside **75–90 min** floor, **2 h** safer if gates are far apart or you need food/lounge time.
- **Separate tickets:** You must exit security, collect bags, and check in again — treat as **3+ hours** minimum between flights.

## Security & Screening Tips
- LAX security can be brutal during peak hours (early morning and 4–8pm). Arrive earlier than you think.
- PreCheck lanes are usually much faster. CLEAR is also available but lines can still form.
- Terminal 4 (Delta) and TBIT often have the most modern checkpoints.
- **Terminal change on separate tickets** always means a **new** TSA line — airside walkways only help when you stay on one ticket and remain airside.

## Best Airport Tricks & Hacks
- **TBIT (Tom Bradley)**: Best lounges and international food options. If you have access to a Star Alliance or oneworld lounge here, use it.
- **Airside terminal hopping**: You can reach T3 Shake Shack from TBIT/T4–T8 without leaving security — but allow **15–25 min** walking for every 1–2 terminals crossed; don't cut a tight connection for a burger.
- **In-N-Out (landside only)**: The famous spot is at **9149 S Sepulveda Blvd** — **outside** security, ~15 min walk from T1 arrivals level via Sepulveda. You cannot reach it airside; only plan this on a long layover where you can exit and re-clear TSA.
- **Shake Shack (airside)**: Terminal **3** near **Gate 34** (next to Starbucks). Order-ahead via the Shake Shack app works here; hours roughly **4:30 a.m.–midnight** per airport operator.
- **Cell phone lot + rideshare timing**: The cell phone lot is well run. For rideshares, sometimes walking one terminal over can save 15–20 minutes during surge.
- **Power & seating**: TBIT has good charging stations and quieter seating on the mezzanine level near gates 150+.
- **Baggage claim trick**: For international arrivals, the first bags often come out at the far end of the carousel. Position yourself accordingly.

## Terminals & Navigation
- **Layout:** Horseshoe of nine passenger terminals around a central roadway — upper level departures, lower level arrivals.
- **Airside (post-security):** Walkways and tunnels connect **T1 through T8 and TBIT** without leaving the secure area. Adjacent terminals are often **5–10 min** on foot; T1↔TBIT or T1↔T8 can run **25–40+ min** depending on gate locations.
- **Landside:** Free **LAX Shuttle Route A** loops all terminals on the lower/arrivals level every **10–15 min** if you must change terminals before security.
- **American Eagle remote gates (52A–52I):** Airside shuttle from T4 gate area — not a simple walk.
- **Common mistakes:** Assuming TBIT is "next door" to your domestic gate (it's often a long airside walk); booking a **45 min** self-transfer on separate tickets (requires exit, bags, new check-in, new TSA).

## Lounges, Food & Amenities
- **Star Alliance / oneworld:** TBIT has the strongest lounge cluster (multiple alliance lounges). Worth the airside walk from T4–T8 if you have time.
- **Delta (T3):** Sky Club in the renovated T3 complex; Shake Shack airside near Gate 34.
- **American (T4):** Admirals Club; T4 connects airside to TBIT for international connections.
- **Food — actually good:**
  - **Shake Shack** — T3 airside, Gate 34 area.
  - **In-N-Out** — landside only at Sepulveda & 92nd; not reachable without exiting security.
  - **TBIT** — widest international dining selection airside.
- **Overrated / traps:** Leaving security for In-N-Out on a sub-2-hour connection; assuming all "LAX terminals" are a short walk (the full airside loop is ~2 miles).
- **Wi‑Fi:** Free airport Wi‑Fi available; session limits vary — re-login if dropped.
- **Water:** Bring an empty bottle; refill stations exist in newer terminal areas — bottled water is standard airport markup.

## Ground Transport & Parking
- **FlyAway buses** run only to **Union Station** (downtown) and **Van Nuys** — board at blue FlyAway columns on the lower/arrivals level at each terminal ([LAX FlyAway](https://www.flylax.com/flyaway)).
- **Metro:** FlyAway to Union Station connects to Metro Rail, Metrolink, and Amtrak.
- **Uber/Lyft:** Pickup at the official **LAX-it** lot — organized but can mean long waits and surge pricing during peaks.
- **Taxi:** Available at each terminal on the lower level; flat rates to some zones — confirm before boarding.
- **Parking:** Central garages are convenient but expensive for multi-day trips — compare long-term/off-site lots and book ahead.
