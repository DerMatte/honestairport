# LAX Airport Guide

> A horseshoe of nine terminals where the wrong assumption about “staying airside” costs you a missed flight — know your pier and which buildings actually connect behind security.

## Quick Facts
- **Terminals:** **1–8** plus **Tom Bradley International Terminal (TBIT)**. Assignments shift with airline moves — confirm on your boarding pass and [flylax.com](https://www.flylax.com/) the day of travel.
- **MCT (same ticket, practical floor):**
  - **Same terminal / connected airside pier (T4–TBIT–T8):** **60–90 min** minimum; add time for TBIT or satellite walks.
  - **Terminal change requiring landside transfer (e.g. T1 → TBIT):** **2+ hours** is sane — shuttle, re-check-in if needed, and **full TSA** again.
  - **International arrival → domestic connection:** immigration, baggage claim, customs, then terminal transfer — **3+ hours** minimum; not a quick airside hop.
- **Separate tickets:** treat as two departures — collect bags if checked, change terminals landside, check in again, clear TSA. **3+ hours** minimum between flights; more in peak TSA windows.
- **Wi‑Fi:** Free airport Wi‑Fi is available — session limits and registration steps vary by terminal; check [flylax.com](https://www.flylax.com/) for current instructions.
- **Liquids (TSA):** **3-1-1** rule — liquids/gels/aerosols in containers **≤3.4 oz (100 ml)** in one quart-sized bag; laptops and large electronics out unless your lane uses CT rules (check TSA guidance).

## Security & Screening Tips
- **Peak pain:** Early morning (**05:00–09:00**) and late afternoon (**16:00–20:00**) routinely produce the longest TSA queues — add **20–30 min** beyond what your airline app shows.
- **PreCheck / CLEAR:** PreCheck lanes are usually faster when open; CLEAR can still queue at peak. Verify which checkpoint your airline uses in your terminal.
- **Terminal quality varies:** Renovations continue (notably **Terminal 5** from late 2025) — checkpoint locations and wait times can shift; use the **LAX app** or official wait-time displays when available.
- **Connection trap:** Airside walkways do **not** reach **Terminal 1** from the T4–TBIT cluster — if your inbound and outbound terminals differ across that split, you **must** go landside and re-screen.
- **International arrivals:** Everyone clears **CBP** (immigration + customs) before any onward domestic flight — connection time must include that, not just walking between gates.

## Best Airport Tricks & Hacks
- **Airside cluster (T4–TBIT–T8):** Post-security walkways (and moving sidewalks at TBIT) let you move among **T4, T5, T6, T7, T8, and TBIT** without exiting TSA — still budget **10–20+ min** of walking depending on gates.
- **TBIT lounge hack:** If you have lounge access at **TBIT** but depart from **T4/T5**, the airside connector makes a TBIT lounge visit realistic on a **90+ min** connection (verify your lounge’s terminal rules).
- **Shake Shack (airside):** Located in **Terminal 3** near **Gate 34** (Delta focus terminal) — order via app at peak meal times; **not** in TBIT despite older blog posts.
- **In-N-Out (landside only):** The famous **In-N-Out at 9149 S Sepulveda Blvd** is **outside** the secure area — you must **exit**, eat, and **re-clear TSA**. Only viable on long layovers with time to spare; **not** an airside meal option.
- **LAX-it for rideshare:** Uber/Lyft use the centralized **LAX-it** lot — take the **green LAX-it shuttle** from the **Lower/Arrivals** level at your terminal; walking to a “closer” terminal rarely beats a full shuttle cycle at peak.
- **Cell phone lot:** Free waiting area for pickups — useful for drivers; passengers should still use LAX-it for app rides.
- **Baggage carousel:** On some international arrivals, bags sometimes cluster at one end of the belt — still watch the full carousel before assuming a miss.

## Terminals & Navigation
- **TBIT:** Primary long-haul international terminal; airside links to **T4** (bridge with moving walkways, ~**5–10 min** walk) and through **T4→T5→T6→T7→T8** tunnels/walkways.
- **T1:** **Southwest** hub — **no airside** link to TBIT/T4 cluster; landside **LAX Shuttle A** (Airline Connections) between terminals.
- **T2/T3:** Delta and partners; **T3** has growing airside connectivity toward **TBIT** as modernization finishes — confirm signage day-of; don’t assume a short walk until connected route is signed.
- **T4–T8:** Mostly connected **airside** in sequence; walking T4→T8 can exceed **15–20 min** without queues.
- **Landside transfers:** **LAX Shuttle A** (green buses, **Lower/Arrivals**) connects all terminals — frequency varies; budget **15–25 min** including wait, longer in traffic around the horseshoe.
- **Recommended connection buffers (practical):**
  - Same ticket, same connected pier (e.g. T5→T5): **60–90 min**
  - Same ticket, airside T4↔TBIT or similar: **90 min** floor, **2 h** comfortable
  - Same ticket, landside terminal change (e.g. T1→TBIT): **2.5–3 h**
  - International → domestic on one ticket: **3 h** floor, **3.5 h+** in peak immigration
  - Separate tickets: **3 h** minimum, **4 h** safer
- **Common mistakes:** Assuming all nine terminals connect airside; underestimating **TBIT** gate distance; leaving for In-N-Out with under **2 hours** before boarding.

## Lounges, Food & Amenities
- **TBIT lounges:** Strongest concentration of **oneworld / Star Alliance / independent** lounges for long-haul — worth positioning if your connection time allows a walk from T4/T5.
- **Terminal 4 (American):** Flagship and Admirals Club options for AA itineraries; food courts skew generic — **order-ahead apps** help at peak.
- **Terminal 3:** **Shake Shack**, **Alfred Coffee**, **Chicken + Beer** airside — best quick-service cluster for Delta passengers.
- **Overrated / traps:** Paying premium for bottled water airside when you had time landside; trekking landside for In-N-Out on a **<2 h** connection; generic “celebrity” sit-down restaurants at rush hour without a reservation or mobile order.
- **Power:** TBIT mezzanine and newer gate areas have solid charging — plug in right after security, not when boarding is called.
- **Water:** Bring an **empty bottle**; refill stations vary by terminal — check signage post-security.

## Ground Transport & Parking
- **FlyAway (official):** Dedicated buses **only** to **LA Union Station** and **Van Nuys** — **365 days/year**, board **Lower/Arrivals** at blue **FlyAway** columns at each terminal ([FlyAway](https://www.flylax.com/flyaway)). Travel time is traffic-dependent (Union Station often **30–60 min**, Van Nuys **40–80 min** per airport FAQ). **Cash not accepted** on board — app, web ticket, or contactless tap; **check current fare** before travel.
- **Metro:** **K Line (Crenshaw)** and bus connections link the airport area to wider LA — good value if you know your route; less ideal with heavy luggage than FlyAway or taxi.
- **Taxi / ride-hail:** Pickups via **LAX-it**; surge pricing common at peak — compare with FlyAway for solo budget trips to downtown.
- **Parking:** Terminal garages are expensive for multi-day trips — compare economy lots and book ahead; **P20-style** short-stay promotions change — verify on [flylax.com](https://www.flylax.com/) if you plan to park and eat landside.
