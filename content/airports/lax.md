# LAX Airport Guide

> A sprawling multi-terminal airport where the hard part is rarely the flight — it's knowing which terminal you're in and whether you can stay airside.

## Quick Facts
- **Layout:** Active CTA terminals: **1, 3, 4, 6, 7, 8**, plus **Tom Bradley International (TBIT / Terminal B)** and **West Gates**. **Terminals 2 and 5 are closed** for redevelopment (airlines relocated — check [LAX airline list](https://www.flylax.com/en/lax-airline-list) before every trip).
- **Major carriers (May 2026):** **JetBlue & Southwest** (T1), **Delta** (T3), **American** (T4), **Alaska & Hawaiian** (T6), **United** (T7–T8); most foreign carriers use **TBIT (B)**.
- **Connections (critical):** **Terminals 6–8 are linked airside** — no re-screening for T6↔T8 connections on one ticket. **TBIT and Terminals 1–3 are not connected airside to T6–8** — you must **exit security**, take the **Pink "A" shuttle** (Lower/Arrivals, ~every 10 min, full loop ~30 min in normal traffic), and **re-clear TSA** at the departure terminal ([LAX FAQ](https://www.flylax.com/lax-frequently-asked-questions-faq)).
- **MCT (practical floor, not airline marketing):**
  - Same terminal / connected airside zone (e.g. T6→T8): **60–90 min**
  - Cross-CTA change (e.g. TBIT→T3): **2.5–3 h minimum** including shuttle and re-screening
  - Separate tickets: **3+ hours** — treat as a self-transfer
- **Wi‑Fi:** **`_LAX Free WiFi`** — select network, watch a short ad (~15–30 s), then unlimited access for the session ([LAX Wi‑Fi](https://www.flylax.com/lax-wifi)).
- **Liquids:** Standard US **3-1-1** rule — quart-sized bag, 100 ml (3.4 oz) containers.

## Security & Screening Tips
- **Peak pain windows (LAX official guidance):** Domestic peaks roughly **06:30–09:00**; international peaks **11:00–14:00** and **20:00–23:00** — arrive earlier than your airline's generic advice during these banks.
- **PreCheck / CLEAR:** Available at multiple checkpoints; PreCheck lanes are usually faster but still queue at peak. CLEAR does not replace TSA identity verification.
- **Cross-terminal trap:** If your inbound is **TBIT** and outbound **T3/T6**, you **will** leave the secure area — budget **45–90 min** for shuttle + security, not walking time alone.
- **Construction:** The CTA is under heavy renovation (Automated People Mover, terminal upgrades) — shuttle routes and curbs shift; check [LAX alerts](https://www.flylax.com/) the day of travel.
- **International arrivals:** All passengers clear **CBP** landside; Mobile Passport Control app can speed entry ([LAX customs page](https://www.flylax.com/lax-customs-and-immigration)).

## Best Airport Tricks & Hacks
- **Pink "A" shuttle first:** For any landside terminal change, board under **PINK LAX Shuttle & Airline Connections** signs on **Lower/Arrivals** — track live shuttles at [ridelax.com](https://www.ridelax.com/).
- **T6–T8 airside shortcut:** If both flights are in the **6–8 cluster**, walk the airside connectors — **do not** exit to the curb unnecessarily.
- **Shake Shack (T3 airside only):** Reliable fast food **after security in Terminal 3** — not at TBIT or T4. Order via app at peak meal times.
- **In-N-Out is not in the terminal:** The famous Sepulveda Blvd location is **outside airport property** (~15 min walk from T1 arrivals) — only viable on long layovers **with** time to exit and re-clear security.
- **TBIT lounges & food:** Strongest international dining and lounge cluster (**Star Alliance**, **oneworld**, **SkyTeam** carriers) — worth routing through if you have lounge access and fly from TBIT.
- **LAX-it for rideshare:** Uber/Lyft/taxi **pickups** are at the **LAX-it lot** beside T1 — take the **free green shuttle** from green **LAX-it** signs on Lower/Arrivals (or walk if light on luggage). Drop-offs remain at terminal departures level.
- **Cell phone lot:** Free waiting on **96th St** east of Sepulveda — better than circling the CTA during construction delays.
- **Avoid:** Assuming "LAX" means one building; booking tight connections across **TBIT ↔ domestic terminals** on separate tickets; expecting In-N-Out airside.

## Terminals & Navigation
- **Tom Bradley (TBIT / B):** Main international terminal with **West Gates** extension — check whether your gate is in the **main TBIT** or **West Gates** (extra walk/tunnel time).
- **Terminal 3:** **Delta** hub; **Shake Shack**, **Alfred Coffee**, and other airside dining.
- **Terminal 4:** **American** and partners (Japan Airlines, British Airways among others per current airline map).
- **Terminals 6–8 cluster:** Connected **airside** — **Alaska/Hawaiian** (T6), **United** (T7–T8).
- **Walking vs. shuttle:** LAWA estimates **10–15 min walk** between adjacent terminals landside; full CTA loop by shuttle ~**30 min** under normal conditions.
- **Recommended connection buffers:**
  - Same connected zone (T6–T8): **60–90 min**
  - TBIT ↔ T3 (limited airside among int'l terminals): **90 min–2 h**
  - TBIT/T1–3 ↔ T6–8: **2.5–3 h** floor
  - Separate tickets: **3+ hours**
- **People Mover (2026+):** LAX/Metro Transit Center connects via free shuttle today; the **Automated People Mover** to terminals is planned to open in **2026** — verify status before assuming a rail-to-gate link exists.

## Lounges Food & Amenities
- **TBIT:** Best lounge density for international carriers — **Star Alliance**, **oneworld**, and **SkyTeam** lounges cluster here. Check alliance status against **operating carrier**, not marketing carrier.
- **Terminal 4:** American **Admirals Club**; partner lounges vary by airline.
- **Terminal 3:** Solid airside food court including **Shake Shack** (6:00–22:00 per concession listings — confirm day-of).
- **TBIT dining:** **Border Grill** and other chef-driven options airside — better than generic CTA fast food if you have time.
- **Power:** Newer gate areas in TBIT West Gates and renovated terminals have USB outlets — charge after security, not during boarding call.
- **Water:** Refill stations vary by terminal; bottled water airside is standard airport pricing — fill an empty bottle landside when fountains are available.
- **Overrated / traps:** Sit-down chains at full menu price during a 45-minute connection; exiting for Sepulveda In-N-Out on a tight international connection.

## Ground Transport & Parking
- **Best value to downtown:** **FlyAway** nonstop bus to **Union Station** or **Van Nuys** — **$12.75** one-way, pay via app/online/contactless (**no cash**); boards Lower/Arrivals at blue **FlyAway** columns ([FlyAway](https://www.flylax.com/flyaway)).
- **Metro rail:** Take free **Metro Connector** shuttle to **LAX/Metro Transit Center** for **C & K Lines** — then B/D/Expo connections in the city ([LAX ground transport](https://www.flylax.com/lax-traffic-and-ground-transportation)).
- **Rideshare / taxi:** Pickups at **LAX-it** (green shuttle from terminals); drop-offs at departures level. Surge pricing hits peak holiday and evening banks.
- **Rental cars:** Off-airport; purple **Rental Car Shuttle** from Lower/Arrivals islands.
- **Parking:** Terminal garages are expensive — **LAX Economy Parking** with pink shuttle is the usual money-saver for multi-day trips.
- **Rush hour reality:** **I-405** and **Century Blvd** clog **07:00–09:30** and **16:00–19:00** — FlyAway or Metro often beats road to downtown in peaks.
