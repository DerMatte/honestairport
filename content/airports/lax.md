# LAX Airport Guide

> Practical, no-fluff information with an emphasis on actually useful tips and tricks.

## Quick Facts
- **Terminals:** 1–8 plus **Tom Bradley International Terminal (TBIT)**. TBIT sits between T3 and T4 and handles most wide-body international flights.
- **Airside connections:** Since 2023, all nine terminals are connected **post-security** via walkways (~2 miles T1 to T8). You can walk between terminals without re-screening if you stay airside.
- **MCT (same ticket):** Same-terminal connections can work in **45–60 min** if gates are close. Cross-terminal airside walks can take **15–30+ minutes** — treat **90 min** as a safer floor when changing terminals.
- **Separate tickets:** You must exit security, collect bags if checked, and check in again — budget **3+ hours**.
- **Wi‑Fi:** Free `_LAX Free WiFi` in all terminal public areas; 45-minute sessions after a short ad (reconnect as needed).
- **Liquids:** Standard TSA **3-1-1** rule — containers 3.4 oz (100 ml) or less in one quart-sized bag.

## Security & Screening Tips
- **Peak pain windows:** Early morning (06:00–09:00) and late afternoon/evening (16:00–20:00) are consistently slow — arrive earlier than you think.
- **PreCheck:** Dedicated lanes at most terminals; usually much faster than standard screening.
- **CLEAR:** Available at T1, T3, T4, T6, T7, and TBIT — can still queue at busy times.
- **T4 (Delta/American) and TBIT** tend to have the most modern checkpoints after recent renovations.
- **International arrivals connecting domestically:** After customs at TBIT you are **outside** security. Use the **connecting flights** door near domestic bag re-check for a dedicated re-screening lane into the TBIT–T4 airside connector (when open — confirm signage the day of travel).

## Best Airport Tricks & Hacks
- **TBIT alliance lounges:** Star Alliance Lounge (Level 6) and oneworld Business Lounge (Level 5) are the standout options for eligible international passengers — reachable airside from any connected terminal.
- **Shake Shack (T3 airside):** Reliable fast food if you are in or can walk to Terminal 3 post-security. Order via app at peak times.
- **In-N-Out is NOT airside:** There is no In-N-Out inside any LAX terminal. The nearest location is at **9149 S Sepulveda Blvd** (~1 mile off-property). Only viable on a **3+ hour landside layover** — walk, rideshare, or shuttle required.
- **LAX-it for rideshare/taxi:** Uber, Lyft, and taxis pick up at **LAX-it** next to T1, not at your arrival terminal curb. Take the free green **LAX-it shuttle** from baggage claim or walk if close.
- **Rideshare drop-off:** Ride apps can drop you at your terminal on the **Upper/Departures** level — pickup is the pain point, not drop-off.
- **Cell phone lot:** The West Cell Phone Lot on 96th Street is the designated free waiting area for drivers picking up passengers.
- **Power & seating:** TBIT West Gates and mezzanine areas near gates 140–159 have charging stations and quieter seating than the main concourse during peaks.

## Terminals & Navigation
- **Layout:** Terminals form a horseshoe (the “U”). TBIT is the international hub in the middle of the south side.
- **Airside walking:** Fully connected since the T3–TBIT walkway opened in 2023. T1↔T8 is roughly **2 miles** — allow **20–40 min** depending on pace and crowds.
- **Landside:** Free **Airline/Terminal Connections (Route A)** shuttle connects all terminals 24/7 if you are not airside.
- **American Eagle remote terminal:** Airside shuttle to/from T4 and T6 only.
- **Recommended connection buffers:**
  - Same terminal: **45–60 min** minimum
  - Airside terminal change: **75–90 min** minimum; **90+ min** safer at peaks
  - Separate tickets / landside transfer: **3+ hours**

## Lounges, Food & Amenities
- **Worth it (eligible passengers):**
  - **Star Alliance Lounge (TBIT, Level 6):** Outdoor terrace, noodle bar — best for Star Alliance international departures.
  - **oneworld Business Lounge (TBIT, Level 5):** Solid option for oneworld business/first and Emerald/Sapphire status.
  - **Delta Sky Club (T3):** Best bet for Delta passengers; day passes subject to availability.
  - **Qantas First Lounge (TBIT, Level 5):** Among the best in the airport for eligible first-class/oneworld Emerald passengers.
- **Food — actually good:**
  - **Shake Shack (T3 airside):** Consistent burgers/shakes; app ordering saves time.
  - **TBIT West Gates:** Chicken Guy!, Santouka ramen, Burger King — mix of sit-down and grab-and-go.
  - **Native by Nyesha (T3 airside):** Healthier sit-down option if you have time.
- **Traps:** Generic chain food at peak meal times without ordering ahead; assuming In-N-Out is inside the airport (it is not).
- **Water:** Refill stations exist in newer terminal areas — bring an empty bottle; bottled water is airport-priced.
- **Power:** Charge right after security, not at the gate during a delay — outlets fill fast.

## Ground Transport & Parking
- **FlyAway bus:** Nonstop service to **Union Station** and **Van Nuys** — **$12.75 one-way**. Pick up on Lower/Arrivals at blue FlyAway columns; drops at Upper/Departures on return to LAX.
- **Metro:** FlyAway to Union Station connects to Metro Rail (B/D lines). Budget **60–90 min** to downtown depending on connections.
- **Uber/Lyft/taxi:** Pickup at **LAX-it** only (free shuttle from terminals). Drop-off at your terminal departures level.
- **Rental cars:** Shuttle buses to consolidated rental car facility (off-airport).
- **Parking:** Terminal parking fills fast — book online in advance for short trips; economy lots P6/P7 with shuttle service for longer stays.
- **Construction:** LAX is mid-transformation (Automated People Mover, terminal upgrades) — check [flylax.com](https://www.flylax.com/) for lane closures and shuttle changes before travel.
